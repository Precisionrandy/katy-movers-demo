Your rights when moving in Texas

Only use a licensed mover

The TxDMV licenses moving companies and provides consumer protection so you can make a smart move. Texas law requires movers to give you a written estimate, a written contract before the move, and a brochure outlining your rights.

  • If a moving deal seems too good to be true, it probably is.
  • Never accept a verbal quote or agreement.
  • Never hire an unlicensed mover — Texas movers must be licensed by the TxDMV.
  • Read the contract before signing it.

More info at TxDMV.gov or call (888) 368-4689.

How this demo works

  1. The quote form sends to a real backend API (/api/quote) — the same payload shape a production system gets from a phone call.
  2. The server scores the lead using real logic: home size, distance, special items, and timing all factor into the estimate.
  3. Speed-to-lead: in production, every captured lead instantly fires a text to the customer and notifies the business owner to call — within seconds, not hours.
  4. Pay-to-confirm: once the owner confirms a quote, a secure payment link goes out so the customer can lock in their date on the spot.
  5. The chat assistant answers questions from the business's own details (service area, pricing, hours) and offers to connect to a live person when it makes sense.
